Log Management Tips

Reduce costs and get more value from your Datadog logs.

Use Exclusion Filters

Filter out noisy, low-value logs at ingestion time. Health checks, debug logs, and repetitive messages can be excluded to dramatically reduce volume.

Set Up Log Pipelines

Parse, enrich, and remap your logs with pipelines. Structured logs are easier to query, take less storage, and enable powerful faceted search.

Archive to Cloud Storage

Route logs to S3, GCS, or Azure Blob for long-term, cost-effective retention. Use Rehydration to bring them back into Datadog when needed.

Use Log-Based Metrics

Generate metrics from log data instead of retaining every log. Track error rates, latencies, and counts without paying for full log storage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the monthly estimate calculated?
We use standard calendar constants: 30.42 days/month (365/12) and 4.33 weeks/month (52/12). Your input value is multiplied by the selected unit (millions, thousands, or raw), then scaled to a monthly rate based on the input period.
What does Datadog charge for log ingestion?
Datadog charges for log ingestion on a per-GB basis. On-demand pricing is approximately $0.10/GB, while committed (annual) plans can bring this down to around $0.05/GB. Pricing may vary by region and contract — always check Datadog's official pricing page for current rates.
What is a retention period?
Retention defines how long Datadog keeps your indexed logs available for search. Shorter retention (3-7 days) costs less but limits your historical search window. Longer retention (30-90 days) provides more history but at a higher cost per GB.
How accurate is the cost estimate?
The cost estimate is approximate and based on the average log size you specify (default 1 KB). Real log sizes vary widely — JSON-heavy application logs may average 2-5 KB, while simple syslog messages might be under 0.5 KB. For accurate forecasting, check your actual average log size in Datadog's Log Explorer.
Can I use this for metrics or APM estimation?
This tool is designed specifically for log ingestion estimation. Datadog metrics and APM (traces) use different pricing models and volume calculations. We may add support for those in the future.
Is this tool affiliated with Datadog?
No. This is an independent, open-source tool created to help engineers estimate their log costs. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Datadog, Inc. in any way.
